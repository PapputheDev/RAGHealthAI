INSURANCE FAQ (Synthetic Example)
Version: 2026-06-02
Clinic Name (Synthetic): Harborview Family & Urgent Care (HFUC)

IMPORTANT NOTE
- Synthetic content for RAG testing only.
- Always verify benefits directly with your insurance plan.

1) QUICK DEFINITIONS
- Copay: fixed amount due at visit (e.g., $30)
- Deductible: amount you pay before insurance starts paying
- Coinsurance: percentage you pay after deductible (e.g., 20%)
- In-network: clinic has a contract with your insurer
- Out-of-network: may cost more and may not be covered

2) COMMON QUESTIONS
Q: Do you accept insurance?
A: HFUC accepts many PPO/HMO plans, but coverage varies. We recommend confirming in-network status.

Q: What do I need to bring?
A: Photo ID, insurance card, and current medication list.

Q: What if my insurance information changes?
A: Update it in the portal before your visit or at check-in.

Q: Do you offer self-pay pricing?
A: Yes. See Appointment Policy for sample self-pay rates.

Q: Can you estimate my cost?
A: We can provide a non-binding estimate based on typical codes, but final patient responsibility depends on your plan.

3) TELEHEALTH COVERAGE
Q: Is telehealth covered?
A: Many plans cover video/phone visits; some apply a different copay than in-person visits.

Q: Does my deductible apply to telehealth?
A: Often yes, depending on plan design.

4) REFERRALS AND AUTHORIZATIONS
Q: Do I need a referral to see a specialist?
A: Some HMO plans require a referral from your PCP.

Q: How long do authorizations take?
A: Commonly 7–14 days, but this varies by payer and service.

5) BILLING AND CLAIMS (Synthetic)
- Claims are typically submitted within 5 business days.
- Patient statements may arrive after insurance processing.
- If insurance denies a claim, you may receive a bill; you can request a review.

6) PAYMENT OPTIONS
- Credit/debit, HSA/FSA cards.
- Payment plans may be available for balances over $250 (synthetic policy).

7) EXAMPLES FOR RAG TESTING
- “My plan is out-of-network. What happens?” -> Higher patient responsibility; verify coverage.
- “I have an HMO. Do I need a referral?” -> Possibly yes.
- “Why did I get a bill weeks later?” -> Insurance processing timelines.
