HIPAA-ALIGNED PRIVACY GUIDELINES (Synthetic Example)
Version: 2026-06-02
Applies To: Harborview Family & Urgent Care (HFUC) staff and systems (synthetic)

IMPORTANT NOTE
- This is synthetic training/test content. It is not legal advice.
- HIPAA rules can be complex; consult compliance professionals for real policies.

1) KEY CONCEPTS
- PHI (Protected Health Information): health information that can identify a person.
- Minimum Necessary: access/use/share only what you need to do your job.
- Authorization: written permission from the patient for certain disclosures.

2) PATIENT RIGHTS (HIGH LEVEL)
Patients generally have the right to:
- Request access to their medical records (with certain limitations)
- Request corrections (amendments)
- Receive an accounting of disclosures (for certain disclosure types)
- Request restrictions on certain uses/disclosures (not always required to agree)
- Choose how they are contacted (reasonable requests)

3) PERMITTED USES/DISCLOSURES (COMMON EXAMPLES)
- Treatment: sharing with other providers involved in care
- Payment: billing, claims, eligibility verification
- Health care operations: quality improvement, audits, training (as allowed)

4) DISCLOSURES THAT TYPICALLY REQUIRE AUTHORIZATION
- Marketing communications (with exceptions)
- Release of records to third parties not involved in care/payment/operations
- Certain non-routine disclosures not otherwise permitted by law

5) SAFEGUARDS
5.1 Administrative
- Role-based access: staff access limited to their job function
- Workforce training: annual privacy/security training
- Incident reporting: immediate reporting of suspected breaches

5.2 Physical
- Locked storage areas for paper records
- Screen privacy: lock screens when stepping away
- Clean desk: avoid leaving PHI unattended

5.3 Technical
- Unique user IDs; strong passwords; MFA where available
- Encryption in transit and at rest when supported
- Audit logging for access to electronic records

6) COMMUNICATION GUIDANCE
- Phone: verify identity using at least two identifiers
- Voicemail: keep messages minimal (no detailed diagnosis)
- Email/text: use secure messaging when possible; inform patients of risks if not secure

7) TELEHEALTH PRIVACY
- Use HIPAA-aligned platforms when feasible.
- Conduct visits from private locations.
- Avoid recording sessions unless clinically necessary and authorized by policy.

8) BREACH RESPONSE (SYNTHETIC WORKFLOW)
- Step 1: Contain (disable accounts, retrieve mis-sent materials)
- Step 2: Assess (what data, how many people, risk of harm)
- Step 3: Notify compliance team and leadership
- Step 4: Notify affected individuals as required
- Step 5: Document and remediate

9) EXAMPLES FOR RAG TESTING
- “What does minimum necessary mean?” -> Limit access to job needs.
- “Can we leave detailed lab results on voicemail?” -> Generally avoid; keep minimal.
- “What are safeguards?” -> Administrative/physical/technical controls.

END OF DOCUMENT
