TELEHEALTH POLICY (Synthetic Example)
Version: 2026-06-02
Clinic Name (Synthetic): Harborview Family & Urgent Care (HFUC)
Applies To: Video and phone visits for primary care and urgent care concerns

IMPORTANT NOTE
- This document is synthetic and for software/RAG testing only.
- It does not replace medical advice. In an emergency, call local emergency services.

1) ELIGIBILITY AND SCOPE
1.1 Appropriate for telehealth
- Minor acute symptoms: cold/flu-like symptoms, mild sore throat, seasonal allergies
- Skin concerns: rashes (non-emergency), acne follow-up, eczema flares
- Chronic condition follow-up: blood pressure check-ins, diabetes education follow-up (non-urgent)
- Lab result review, medication counseling (non-controlled substances)
- Mental health check-ins (non-crisis)

1.2 Not appropriate for telehealth (require in-person evaluation)
- Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, fainting, confusion, stroke-like symptoms
- Severe abdominal pain, uncontrolled bleeding, suspected fracture with deformity
- High fever with stiff neck, severe dehydration, or infant < 3 months with fever
- Any concern that requires hands-on exam, procedure, imaging, or immediate vitals

2) VISIT MODALITIES
2.1 Video visit (preferred)
- Requires camera-enabled device and stable internet.
- Patient must be physically located in a state where HFUC clinicians are licensed.

2.2 Phone visit
- Used when video is not possible.
- May be limited for issues requiring visual inspection.

3) CONSENT AND IDENTITY
- Patient must provide verbal consent at visit start.
- Clinician may request two identifiers (e.g., full name + date of birth).
- For minors: a parent/guardian must participate unless legally allowed otherwise.

4) PRIVACY AND ENVIRONMENT
- Patients should join from a private location.
- Avoid public Wi‑Fi when possible.
- HFUC uses a HIPAA-aligned telehealth platform when feasible; if a fallback is needed (e.g., phone), limitations are explained.

5) SCHEDULING AND CHECK-IN
- Arrive (log in) 5 minutes early.
- Complete eCheck-in forms: symptoms, medication list, allergies, pharmacy preference.
- Upload photos when requested (e.g., rash). Use good lighting and include a size reference (coin or ruler).

6) CLINICAL LIMITATIONS
- Telehealth is based on information available at the time.
- Clinicians may convert to in-person evaluation if needed.
- Prescriptions may be limited by clinical judgment, legal requirements, and safety.

7) PRESCRIBING RULES
7.1 General
- No guarantee of prescription issuance.
- Antibiotics: prescribed only when clinically appropriate.

7.2 Controlled substances
- HFUC does not initiate new long-term controlled substances via telehealth.
- For refills of existing controlled meds, an in-person visit may be required.

8) DOCUMENTATION AND FOLLOW-UP
- Visit summary is provided within 24 hours.
- If symptoms worsen, patient should seek in-person care.

9) FEES AND BILLING (Synthetic)
- Telehealth copays/deductibles depend on insurance plan.
- Self-pay rates:
  - Video visit (15–20 min): $85
  - Phone visit (10–15 min): $65
- No-show fee: $25 (waived for documented technical failures).

10) TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS & SUPPORT
- Recommended: Chrome/Edge browser, updated OS.
- Troubleshooting steps:
  1) Switch networks
  2) Reconnect audio/video
  3) Use phone backup line
- Support (synthetic): helpdesk@hfuc.example | (555) 010-2000

11) EXAMPLES FOR RAG TESTING (SCENARIOS)
- “I have a rash and can upload photos; can I do telehealth?” -> Yes, likely.
- “I feel chest tightness and can’t catch my breath” -> Not appropriate; emergency evaluation.
- “Can I get my blood pressure medication refilled via video?” -> Often yes if stable, per refill policy.
