Clinic Name (Synthetic): Harborview Family & Urgent Care (HFUC)

IMPORTANT NOTE
- Synthetic content for RAG testing only.
- These are general educational instructions and not individualized medical advice.
- If symptoms worsen or you feel unsafe, seek urgent or emergency care.

1) GENERAL POST-VISIT CARE
- Rest and hydrate.
- Follow the clinician’s instructions and medication labels.
- Avoid driving/operating heavy machinery if taking sedating medications.
- Keep a list of medications and allergies up to date.

2) WHEN TO SEEK URGENT CARE OR EMERGENCY HELP
Seek immediate care for:
- Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, fainting, new confusion
- Severe allergic reaction: swelling of face/lips/tongue, trouble breathing
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Signs of stroke: facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty

3) COMMON CONDITIONS (GENERAL GUIDANCE)
3.1 Viral upper respiratory infection (URI)
- Typical duration: several days to ~2 weeks.
- Supportive care: fluids, rest, humidifier.
- Return/seek care if: persistent high fever, worsening shortness of breath, dehydration.

3.2 Gastroenteritis (stomach bug)
- Focus on hydration: small sips frequently.
- Return/seek care if: blood in stool, severe abdominal pain, inability to keep fluids down.

3.3 Minor musculoskeletal strain
- Rest, gradual return to activity.
- Ice for first 24–48 hours, then heat if helpful.
- Return/seek care if: severe pain, numbness/weakness, visible deformity.

4) MEDICATION SAFETY REMINDERS
- Take medications exactly as directed.
- Do not share prescriptions.
- Check for duplicate ingredients (e.g., acetaminophen in multiple products).
- Contact your clinician or pharmacist if you have side effects.

5) FOLLOW-UP INSTRUCTIONS (SYNTHETIC)
- Follow up with primary care in 7–14 days if symptoms persist.
- Lab/imaging results are typically released to the portal within 1–3 business days.

6) RETURN TO WORK/SCHOOL (GENERAL)
- Return when fever-free for 24 hours without fever-reducing medications and symptoms are improving.
- Follow employer/school policies.
